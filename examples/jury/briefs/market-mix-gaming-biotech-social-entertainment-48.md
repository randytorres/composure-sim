# Market Mix: Gaming, Biotech, Social, Entertainment

Goal: start over from taste and market signals, not from the old generic consumer-AI
list. This slate mixes existing founder-fit sparks with new concepts across games,
biotech/body data, social rituals, entertainment formats, AGON/The Escape style live
seasons, and proof artifacts.

## Research Signals

- **Games are social platforms now.** Roblox reported 85.3M average daily active users
  and 18.7B hours engaged in Q4 2024, with bookings up 21% year over year in its 2024
  shareholder letter. That supports ideas where the product is a place, ritual, or
  playable social object rather than a one-off generated asset.
- **Creator-made game worlds are real markets.** Epic says the Fortnite ecosystem paid
  creators $352M in 2023 and $352M again in 2024, and UEFN keeps expanding the surface
  for creator-built islands. This supports creator/game hybrids, but only if the output
  is actually playable or earns status.
- **Discord is becoming a game/app surface.** Discord's Embedded App SDK lets developers
  build Activities that run inside voice/text contexts. That makes chat-native games,
  quests, tribunals, and squad seasons much more plausible than standalone social apps.
- **Short-form entertainment still wants artifacts.** TikTok's official trend work keeps
  emphasizing community, curiosity, search behavior, and creator-native formats. Ideas
  should create something that can be watched, clipped, argued over, or turned into a
  series.
- **Consumer health is moving from advice to continuous evidence.** FDA cleared Dexcom
  Stelo as the first over-the-counter integrated continuous glucose monitor in 2024.
  Oura has pushed health-signal features like Symptom Radar, and WHOOP has moved into
  Healthspan/lab-style longitudinal tracking. The opportunity is not generic health
  coaching; it is body evidence, anomaly explanation, privacy, and experiment design.
- **AI companions and social simulation need guardrails.** Character/companion safety
  scrutiny and FTC attention around AI companions make open-ended emotional chat a risky
  wedge. Better: bounded social games, fictionalized simulations, receipts, moderation,
  and explicit no-diagnosis/no-real-person-harassment rules.
- **Immersive entertainment keeps proving people pay for shared missions.** Escape rooms,
  location-based entertainment, Meow Wolf-style worlds, and Sandbox VR-style venues show
  demand for group rituals. The software wedge is not "VR venue"; it is a repeatable
  mission engine, case file, squad proof feed, or finale artifact.

## Design Bias

Prefer concepts with:

- one first scene or first artifact
- social consequence or body consequence
- a receipt people can forward, debate, collect, or keep
- a clear first audience
- a prototype that can be faked manually before a platform exists

Avoid concepts that are only:

- "AI game generator"
- wellness dashboard
- generic creator tool
- broad marketplace
- companion chatbot
- media generator with no underlying event/state

## 48 Mixed Ideas

### Gaming And Story Worlds

1. **REALM Oath Court Mini** - Five nobles, one oath, one witness, one hidden grudge,
   one betrayal, and one Daily Raven receipt. First audience: strategy/narrative players.
2. **REALM Witness Market** - Promises require witnesses; witnesses can be bribed,
   protected, discredited, or exposed. First audience: diplomacy/social deduction fans.
3. **PARAGON Civilian Triage Crisis** - One superhero mission where every save changes
   trust, faction power, identity exposure, and civilian harm. First audience: superhero
   game fans who care about consequence.
4. **STARLIGHT Rival District Week** - Idol/anime crews compete for district reputation,
   rehearsal slots, scandals, and fan status. First audience: anime management fans.
5. **CARTEL Heat Ledger** - Anti-glamour logistics strategy about routes, family pressure,
   informants, enforcement heat, and irreversible betrayal. First audience: dark strategy
   players.
6. **THE SHOP After Midnight** - One late shift in a neighborhood shop with supplier
   failure, regulars, inspection risk, cash pressure, and one illegal favor. First
   audience: job-sim players.
7. **Tow Yard Cold Dispatch** - Dispatch tow calls while cops, angry owners, impound
   rules, repair favors, and cash shortages pressure each choice. First audience:
   Papers Please / Not Tonight fans.
8. **Airport Ramp Black Box** - One dangerous ramp shift where weather, baggage, crews,
   maintenance, passenger pressure, and shortcut temptation produce an incident report.
   First audience: logistics/job-sim fans.
9. **Funeral Home Ledger** - A careful job sim about logistics, grief, ritual, family
   conflict, errors, and dignity. First audience: narrative sim players.
10. **Private Security Incident Log** - Night patrol sim where uncertain threats, radio
    calls, cameras, and reports become the core game artifact. First audience: horror/job
    sim players.
11. **The Witness Board** - Social deduction where every claim needs a witness and every
    witness can sell, distort, or reveal the receipt. First audience: Discord/social
    deduction groups.
12. **Creator Drop Arcade: Proof Mode** - Creators launch playable fan challenges; the
    system mines the best runs into clips, winner cards, and sponsor-safe proof. First
    audience: creator managers and streamers.

### Biotech And Body Evidence

13. **Bloodwork Mystery Board** - Labs, wearables, symptoms, and protocol changes become
    a source-backed case board with open questions, not diagnosis. First audience:
    biohackers paying for Function/InsideTracker-style panels.
14. **CGM Meal Boss Battles** - Users run meal experiments as quests: prediction, glucose
    curve, sleep/recovery context, and a shareable "boss defeated or not" card. First
    audience: OTC CGM users and GLP-1 users.
15. **GLP-1 Muscle Shield League** - Protein, lifting, side effects, strength retention,
    and body-composition proof for people on GLP-1s who fear losing muscle. First
    audience: month-3 to month-9 GLP-1 users.
16. **Symptom Radar Household Log** - Private family illness/wellness anomaly cards that
    separate "probably tired" from "pattern worth watching" without diagnosis. First
    audience: parents and wearable owners.
17. **Supplement Court Of Claims** - Every stack claim gets mechanistic evidence, human
    data quality, interaction cautions, cost, and "what would falsify this" cards. First
    audience: skeptical biohackers.
18. **Hormone Protocol Replay** - TRT/HRT/peptide users track dose, labs, symptoms,
    sleep, training, and side effects as a clinician-readable timeline. First audience:
    serious protocol users with providers.
19. **Sleep Debt Escape Room** - Friend/squad challenge where sleep, alcohol, late meals,
    training, and stress become a weekly escape puzzle with proof and penalties. First
    audience: WHOOP/Oura owners who like competition.
20. **Lab Anomaly Interviewer** - A calm agent asks the 12 questions a good clinician or
    coach would ask after an abnormal marker, then creates a visit-prep packet. First
    audience: people with confusing labs.
21. **Microbiome Field Notes** - Diet changes, travel, antibiotics, symptoms, and test
    results become field logs and hypothesis cards. First audience: gut-health obsessives.
22. **Body Data Black Box** - When a wearable score crashes, it reconstructs the likely
    causal chain from sleep, food, stress, training, illness, and context. First audience:
    data-heavy athletes.
23. **Family Health Evidence Vault** - Source-linked medication/lab/symptom packets for
    families helping parents navigate appointments, not a chatbot doctor. First audience:
    adult children and caregivers.
24. **Biohacker Benchmark Dossier** - Protocol experiments become beautiful before/after
    dossiers with methods, confounders, costs, side effects, and uncertainty. First
    audience: prosumer health creators.

### Social Rituals And Status Systems

25. **AGON Seven-Day Escape Pilot** - Seven days, three-person squads, proof missions,
    public scoreboard, captain pressure, and one final in-person task. First audience:
    capability/cohort buyers.
26. **The Escape City Keys** - A city-based season where teams unlock physical locations,
    social tasks, endurance challenges, and proof cards. First audience: run clubs,
    founder groups, and friend crews.
27. **Reputation Weather Room** - Reputation shifts for fictional creators/companies/
    factions visualized as weather fronts with evidence and counter-moves. First audience:
    creators and strategy-game fans.
28. **Court Of Public Opinion: Fiction Mode** - Players argue a fictional scandal using
    receipts; jurors explain what would change their mind. First audience: debate/social
    deduction communities.
29. **Friend Group Newspaper With Receipts** - Private weekly newspaper from approved
    photos, polls, jokes, claims, apologies, plans, and running lore. First audience:
    friend groups and Discord servers.
30. **Discord Quest Tribunal** - Communities run weekly quests, disputes, appeals,
    badges, and recap posts inside Discord. First audience: mods of active niche servers.
31. **Local Rivalry Season** - Coffee shops, gyms, crews, clubs, or bands compete through
    missions, relics, proof cards, and public standings. First audience: local creators
    with community pull.
32. **Civic Quest League Dark Mode** - Residents complete civic missions, document broken
    systems, and earn public proof without partisan sludge. First audience: local civic
    obsessives.
33. **Street Vendor Route Season** - Vendors juggle permits, weather, inventory, cops,
    regulars, and route risk as a sim/tool hybrid. First audience: street vendors and
    job-sim fans.
34. **Campus Club Politics** - Student orgs fight over budgets, rooms, events,
    reputations, alliances, and receipts. First audience: college Discord communities.
35. **Family Council Trial** - Disputed family stories, care duties, inheritance issues,
    and old promises become private case files with uncertainty labels. First audience:
    families dealing with messy coordination.
36. **Local Relic Pass** - Bands, venues, teams, and creators issue collectible proof
    cards tied to real events and missions. First audience: local music/venue scenes.

### Entertainment Formats

37. **Playable Trailer Lab** - A film, album, game, or product pitch becomes a 60-second
    playable scene with replay stats and shareable output. First audience: indie creators
    and studios.
38. **Episode Aftermath Cards** - YouTube/Twitch/podcast episodes produce claim cards,
    faction polls, evidence links, character/status updates, and fan missions. First
    audience: creators with lore-heavy communities.
39. **Fandom Relic Trials** - Fans earn permanent cards/relics through puzzles,
    watch-party proof, attendance, and creator challenges. First audience: musicians,
    streamers, anime communities.
40. **Creator Feud Simulator: Safe Fiction** - Fictionalized PR blowups where creators
    test fan factions, receipts, apologies, and escalation without targeting real people.
    First audience: creator managers.
41. **Watch Party Prediction Court** - Viewers predict outcomes, defend evidence, form
    factions, and get post-episode scorecards. First audience: reality TV/anime/sports
    watch parties.
42. **Local Mystery Case Walks** - Local history, architecture, shops, and archives become
    clue walks ending in a collectible case card. First audience: date-night and local
    tourism.
43. **Skywatch Unknown Card** - Film a weird sky object and receive a source-backed card
    with likely candidates, confidence, and what cannot be known. First audience:
    plane-spotters, UFO-curious skeptics, parents.
44. **SPECTER RF Ghost Replay** - Strange RF/noise/drone/signal events become replayable
    evidence packets with assumptions and provenance. First audience: ham radio, drone,
    and security hobbyists.
45. **Pawn Shop Intelligence** - Strange resale objects get provenance, watchlist,
    repairability, price, and story cards. First audience: pawn/thrift/resale operators.
46. **Cemetery Story Ledger** - Source-linked cemetery/local-history walks with family-
    safe stories, restoration hooks, and memory cards. First audience: local history and
    genealogy groups.
47. **Weather Witness Packets** - Storm/flood/heat/damage reports become timestamped
    evidence cards with source feeds and neighbor corroboration. First audience:
    homeowners, renters, neighborhood groups.
48. **Immersive Night Operator** - Small venues run one-night playable missions with
    roles, clues, proof cards, finale photos, and optional sponsor drops. First audience:
    escape rooms, bars, museums, and microvenues.
